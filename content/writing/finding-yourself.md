A fox stands in front of a long black curtain that cuts the hall it is
using tonight in half. *Welcome, again, to my exhibition,* it says. *I
am Dear, Also, The Tree That Was Felled of the Ode clade, and I have
brought you all here to find yourselves.*

The chatter among the mingling attendees quiets as they notice the
exhibition proper is starting.

*You have perhaps been wondering what I mean by this. Will I provide
grand revelations? Guide you to profound realizations through some
elaborate play? Teach you to meditate?*

Dear pauses for effect.

*No. No. Too fucking metaphorical.*

*Half an hour ago, I asked all of you to send a fork backstage with me.
While you mingled, I had your forks fork again, and then coached your
more distant up-trees through the process of becoming fetching fennec
foxes like myself, though without speech. Once I remove this curtain,
your task is simple: find yourself.*

*I only request that both sides refrain from shouting names, checking
signifiers, or otherwise communicating their way out of the problem. It
would cheapen the exercise.*

*Even with that restriction, it should be easy, yes? You are finding*
yourself. *Surely, you can understand what choices you would have -
did - make backstage as you became a fox without knowing why? You can
recognize the features you kept despite the species change? How much you
copied off me? How small? How large the ears? How you are acting now?*

*Or, perhaps, half an hour in a different context, different bodies, is
too wide a gulf to guess at. But then, even if you should find your
up-tree ... have you found yourself at all?*

*I do not know, and I bid you find out. I, Dear, Also, The Tree That Was
Felled of the Ode clade -*

It pulls a rope that had been dangling behind it, making the curtain
vanish. Many fennecs stand there: some sitting patiently, some nibbling
on refreshments, some frolicking about. *- welcome you, my dear
audience? Cast? Raw materials? to an exhibit about finding yourself.*
