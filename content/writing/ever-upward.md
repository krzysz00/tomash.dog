As Charlie crested the last ridge, stepping out onto an intentionally
crisp, calm, sunny summit from an equally intentional slog through a
biting wind, he smiled. The summit of Mt. Madeline, one of the System's
better-known (if not often climbed all the way) ‘engineered’ mountains,
was as strikingly beautiful and rewarding as it had been on his first
ascent a century and a half ago.

He’d looked (and felt) young and fit and energetic back then, despite
his fifty previous years on the System. Now, Charlie looked old and
frail and tired, and felt even older than he looked.

He looked at the gaggle of family, ex-partners (both climbing and
romantic), and other friends he'd invited here. The gathered friends had
mostly popped right onto the summit, having obtained special permission
from the mountain’s maintainers for this get-together. Many of Charlie's
friends had decided they didn't want to come, didn't want to be around
for this, but those who came were enough.

“Looks like I'm ten minutes late, just like I was for uploading,”
Charlie said, chuckling. “The more things change, I guess.”

The peak grew even quieter as his friends noticed Charlie was here. The
old climber sank down onto a convenient rock, from which he looked at
these people he'd spent two pleasant centuries with.

“We are gathered here today —” Charlie began. Then, he cut himself off.
“No, I can't do it like that. That's too cheesy, even for me.”

“That’s a first,” a snow leopard heckled.

“Says the lady who worsened my pun infection,” Charlie quipped back.
Uncharacteristically nervous laughter broke out within the group.

Once everyone had settled back down, Charlie started over. “Right, so,
thanks for coming, everyone. It's nice to see all of you one last time.

“It's been a great two hundred years since I uploaded, and it's been
made even greater by having all of you in it, even if we've had our
rough patches now and then.” Charlie smiled at everyone.

“But it's been two hundred whole years,” Charlie continued, more
somberly. “I thought this over a lot of times while I climbed up here,
and I'm still sure this’ll be the last time I do that.

“I've had a great run. I've done the things I want to do, met all sorts
of different, wonderful people, and climbed a whole lot of mountains.

“And, for a while now, I've realized … I'm done. I'm good. There's
nothing I want that I haven't gotten around to.”

Charlie paused. “I can't think of anything I'd regret not being here for
enough that I'm up to waiting for it. Heck, if they announce downloading
or something tomorrow and I'll have just missed it … I'm fine with that.
My life's gone on long enough for my liking.”

Charlie stood and looked up at the sky. “So it's time for me to keep
going up, to see what's above the System.”

“Thank you all again for coming to see me off as I leave the best
mountain on the System, and, well, everything else here, behind,”
Charlie continued, holding up a hand to hold off objections to his
choice of best mountain that wouldn't come, just this once. “Please try
to keep the rest of today from being too much of a downer, if you can —
I know you'll miss me, you've said as much, but … I'm tired, I'm ready
to go, and you'll still have your memories.”

Charlie let a silence fall over the peak and the gathering as he walked
to the edge of the summit. “Maybe I'll see you again. Maybe not. Either
way … goodbye.”

With that, Charlie quit, leaving no forks behind.

His tombstone still adorns the peak.

*Here quit Charlie Evans, pioneer of sys-side mountaineering and friend
to many. 43+162.54 — 243+162.51*
